The Kiss

by Anne Sexton

My mouth BLOOMS like a cut.
I've been wronged all year, TEDIOUS
nights, nothing but ROUGH elbows in them
and DELICATE boxes of Kleenex calling

crybaby, crybaby, you fool!



Before today my body was USELESS.
Now it's tearing at its square corners.
It's tearing old Mary's garments OFF,


knot by knot

and see-- Now it's shot full of these electric bolts

Zing! A resurrection!



Once it was a boat quite wooden
and with no business, no salt water under it
and in need of some paint. It was no more
than a group of boards. But you HOISTED her, RIGGED her.
SHE'S BEEN ELECTED NOW

My nerves are turned on. I hear them like
musical instruments. When there was silence
the drums, the strings are INCURABLY playing.

YOU DID THIS.

PURE GENIUS AT WORK.

Darling, the composer has stepped into FIRE.
